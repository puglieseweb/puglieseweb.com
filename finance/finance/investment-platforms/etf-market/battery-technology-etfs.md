# Battery Technology ETFs

ETFs available to UK investors:

**1. L\&G Battery Value-Chain UCITS ETF (BATG/BATT)**

* **Ticker**: BATG (London Stock Exchange)
* **ISIN**: IE00BF0M2Z96
* **Expense Ratio**: 0.49% per year
* **Fund Size**: \~£600 million
* **Index**: Tracks the Solactive Battery Value-Chain Index
* **Strategy**: Covers the entire battery value chain - from mining companies producing battery metals (lithium, cobalt, nickel) to companies developing battery technology and manufacturing batteries
* **Dividends**: Accumulating (reinvested)
* **Availability**: Widely available through UK platforms like Hargreaves Lansdown, Fidelity, and others
* **Holdings**: Includes companies like Tesla, Toyota, and various mining companies

**2. Global X Lithium & Battery Tech ETF (LITG)**

* **Index**: Tracks Solactive Global Lithium v2 Index
* **Focus**: Lithium mining, refining, and battery production
* **Expense Ratio**: 0.75%
* **Fund Size**: Over £5 billion (one of the largest battery ETFs globally)
* **Top Holdings**: Albemarle Corp (\~25%), Ganfeng Lithium, Yunnan Energy
* **Note**: This is a US-domiciled ETF but accessible to UK investors through some platforms

**3. Amplify Lithium & Battery Technology ETF (BATT)**

* **Index**: Tracks EQM Lithium & Battery Technology Index
* **Expense Ratio**: 0.59%
* **Fund Size**: \~$200 million (smaller fund)
* **Holdings**: Tesla (7%), Contemporary Amperex, BHP Group, lithium miners
* **Note**: US-domiciled, available on some UK platforms

**Key Considerations:**

For UK investors, the **L\&G Battery Value-Chain UCITS ETF** is typically the most accessible and tax-efficient option since it's UCITS-domiciled (Ireland) and trades on the London Stock Exchange. UCITS funds generally have better tax treatment for UK investors compared to US-domiciled ETFs.

The sector has seen significant growth due to electric vehicle adoption and renewable energy storage demand, but it's also quite volatile and heavily concentrated in materials and technology sectors.
